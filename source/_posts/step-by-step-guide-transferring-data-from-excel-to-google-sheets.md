 Did someone send you an Excel document, but you don’t have Excel? Giving up on Microsoft Office and making the change to Google Drive? No problem; Google Sheets lets you import your Excel files. And while it might not support some of the same features and effects of an Excel document, it works pretty well.

##  How to Import an Excel Document into Google Sheets

 To view an Excel document on Google Sheets, you must first upload the file to your Google Drive. Open [Google Drive](https://drive.google.com/drive/my-drive), click “New,” and then click “File Upload” to get started.

 Navigate to your file(s) and then click “Open.”

![/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2018/12/2018-12-14_12h06_32.png](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2018/12/2018-12-14_12h06_32.png) 

 Alternatively, you can drag and drop a file from your computer directly into the web browser for an easy upload.

![/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2018/12/2018-12-14_12h07_40.png](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2018/12/2018-12-14_12h07_40.png) 

 Once your file uploads, right-click it, point to “Open With” on the context menu, and then select “Google Sheets.”
